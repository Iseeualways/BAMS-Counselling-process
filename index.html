All India NEET UG BAMS Counselling 2026 Stages

The All India BAMS counselling process conducted by the Ayush Admissions Central Counselling Committee (AACCC) consists of multiple stages. While the counselling schedule varies for each round, the overall process generally remains the same. Before the commencement of every counselling round, AACCC publishes the seat matrix, schedule, and other important notifications on its official website.

1

Stage 1: Registration

Candidates who wish to participate in the All India BAMS counselling process must register online through the official AACCC portal during the registration window of the respective counselling round.

Fresh registration is generally required for each eligible counselling phase except where AACCC specifically carries forward registrations, such as certain phases of the Stray Vacancy Round, subject to the counselling guidelines issued for that admission year.
2

Stage 2: Payment of Counselling Fees

After completing registration, candidates must pay the prescribed counselling fee online.

The counselling fee consists of:

  • Non-Refundable Registration Fee
  • Refundable Security Deposit
Counselling Category Category Registration Fee Refundable Security Deposit
AIQ Government Colleges, Government Aided Colleges, Central Universities & National Institutes UR EWS OBC NCL Rs. 1,000 Rs. 20,000
AIQ Government Colleges, Government Aided Colleges, Central Universities & National Institutes SC ST PwBD Rs. 500 Rs. 20,000
Deemed Universities All Categories Rs. 5,000 Rs. 50,000

Note: Candidates opting for both Government and Deemed University counselling must pay the higher applicable fee.

3

Stage 3: Choice Filling

Registered candidates must select their preferred BAMS colleges and arrange them in the order of preference. Candidates may add, modify, delete, or rearrange their choices until the choice filling window closes.

Choice filling is conducted separately for every counselling round. Choices submitted in one round are generally not carried forward to the subsequent rounds unless specifically notified by AACCC.
4

Stage 4: Choice Locking

Once the preferred colleges have been selected, candidates must lock their choices before the deadline. After locking, the selected choices cannot be modified for that counselling round.

If a candidate does not manually lock the choices before the deadline, AACCC may automatically lock the last saved choices.
5

Stage 5: Seat Allotment

Candidates who are not allotted a seat in one round may participate in subsequent counselling rounds, subject to AACCC eligibility rules.

AACCC processes seat allotment based on multiple factors, including:
  • NEET UG 2026 Rank
  • Candidate Category
  • Reservation Policy
  • Availability of Seats
  • Order of College Preferences
6

Stage 6: Seat Allotment Result

AACCC publishes the seat allotment results on its official website after completing the allotment process. Candidates can download their allotment letter and proceed with the next admission formalities if a seat has been allotted.

Note : AACCC may publish a provisional result before releasing the final seat allotment result whenever required.
7

Stage 7: Reporting to the Allotted College

Candidates allotted a seat must report to the respective college within the prescribed reporting period.

    The admission process generally includes:
  • Document Verification
  • Candidate Category
  • Reservation Policy
  • Availability of Seats
  • Order of College Preferences
Candidates report to the designated college in person, submit required documents, and pay admission fees. The authoritative body verifies documents to confirm the seat. Seats of candidates who don't report within the stipulated time are considered vacant for the next round.

Karnataka AYUSH Counselling 2026

The Karnataka AYUSH counselling process is conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) for admission to AYUSH courses across the state. Candidates must qualify NEET UG 2026 and complete the KEA counselling registration to participate in the admission process.

Conducted by KEA, Government of Karnataka
Eligibility NEET UG 2026 + KEA Registration

KEA conducts counselling for

Government Quota seats in Government AYUSH colleges
Private Quota seats in Private AYUSH colleges
Management Quota seats in Private AYUSH colleges
NRI Quota seats in Private AYUSH colleges
Candidates must register through the official KEA counselling portal and complete the prescribed admission formalities within the notified schedule to become eligible for seat allotment.

KEA NEET UG AYUSH Counselling 2026 Stages

The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) conducts the AYUSH counselling process through multiple stages. Candidates must complete each stage within the prescribed timeline to remain eligible for seat allotment.

1

Stage 1: Online Registration

The counselling process begins with online registration on the official KEA website. Registration is considered complete only after the successful submission of the application and payment of the counselling fee.

Candidates must:
  • Register using the prescribed application form.
  • Enter the required personal, academic, and NEET UG details.
  • Upload the necessary documents, if applicable.
  • Pay the prescribed counselling fee within the specified deadline.
2

Stage 2: Document Verification

After registration, eligible candidates must complete document verification as per the schedule announced by KEA. During this stage, the authorities verify the candidate's eligibility, reservation claims, and supporting documents.

Candidates who successfully complete the verification process receive a verification acknowledgement, enabling them to participate in the subsequent counselling stages.
3

Stage 3: Option Entry

Verified candidates can participate in the option entry process. The order of preferences plays an important role in determining seat allotment.

During this stage, candidates must:
  • Select their preferred AYUSH colleges and courses.
  • Arrange the selected options in the order of preference.
  • Modify, add, or delete choices within the option entry period.
4

Stage 4: Mock Seat Allotment

Before the final allotment, KEA generally publishes a mock seat allotment based on the options entered by candidates.

KEA publishes mock allotment results based on current preferences, helping candidates understand which seats they might be eligible for and reorder accordingly. Results can be viewed by entering your CET number on the KEA website. Final results may differ from the mock allotment.

The mock allotment enables candidates to:

  • Understand their probable allotment.
  • Analyse their admission chances.
  • Revise their option entry before the final seat allotment.

The mock allotment is indicative in nature and does not guarantee the final allotment.

5

Stage 5: Final Seat Allotment

Following the option entry period, KEA publishes the final seat allotment results. Candidates can download their allotment letter through the KEA portal.

Seats are allotted based on rank/merit, preferences, and government reservation rules. Every round follows the same 3-phase structure — see the breakdown below.

Seat allotment is based on factors such as:

  • NEET UG Rank
  • Candidate Category
  • Reservation Policy
  • Availability of Seats
  • Order of Preferences
6

Stage 6: Post Allotment Process

Candidates allotted a seat must complete the admission formalities within the prescribed deadline. Failure to complete the required formalities within the specified timeline may result in cancellation of the allotted seat.

The process in this stage includes:

  • Selecting the appropriate admission option as notified by KEA.
  • Payment of the prescribed fees.
  • Reporting to the allotted college.
  • Completion of document verification and admission formalities at the institute.

Note: KEA follows a structured seat allotment methodology based on merit, reservation policies, category wise seat availability, and candidate preferences. The detailed allotment procedure is published in the official UGNEET Information Bulletin for the respective admission year.
