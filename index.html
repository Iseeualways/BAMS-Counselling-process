All India NEET UG BAMS Counselling 2026 Stages

The All India BAMS counselling process conducted by the Ayush Admissions Central Counselling Committee (AACCC) consists of multiple stages. While the counselling schedule varies for each round, the overall process generally remains the same. Before the commencement of every counselling round, AACCC publishes the seat matrix, schedule, and other important notifications on its official website.

1

Stage 1: Registration

Candidates who wish to participate in the All India BAMS counselling process must register online through the official AACCC portal during the registration window of the respective counselling round.

Fresh registration is generally required for each eligible counselling phase except where AACCC specifically carries forward registrations, such as certain phases of the Stray Vacancy Round, subject to the counselling guidelines issued for that admission year.
2

Stage 2: Payment of Counselling Fees

After completing registration, candidates must pay the prescribed counselling fee online.

The counselling fee consists of:

  • Non-Refundable Registration Fee
  • Refundable Security Deposit
Counselling Category Category Registration Fee Refundable Security Deposit
AIQ Government Colleges, Government Aided Colleges, Central Universities & National Institutes UR EWS OBC NCL Rs. 1,000 Rs. 20,000
AIQ Government Colleges, Government Aided Colleges, Central Universities & National Institutes SC ST PwBD Rs. 500 Rs. 20,000
Deemed Universities All Categories Rs. 5,000 Rs. 50,000

Note: Candidates opting for both Government and Deemed University counselling must pay the higher applicable fee.

3

Choice-Filling

List your course and college preferences — this resets every round.

Candidates opt for the BAMS course and desired colleges in order of preference, editable while the window is open. Choices reset each round: Round 1 choices don't carry into Round 2, and Rounds 1–3 choices become void in SVR-1. This stage doesn't apply to SVR-2 (choices carry forward from SVR-1) or SVR-DU. Eligible candidates approach deemed universities directly for the online SVR-DU round.
4

Choice-Locking

Finalise your choices — once locked, they can't be changed for that round.

Candidates must finalise selected choices. Once locked, choices cannot be changed for the respective round. Choices are auto-locked for anyone who doesn't lock within the given time frame. Like Stage 3, this doesn't apply to SVR-2 or SVR-DU.
5

Seat Allotment Process

AACCC assigns course and college based on score, seats and category.

Allotment depends on:
  • Candidate's NEET score
  • Availability of seats in preferred choices
  • Candidate category
Candidates not allotted a seat in the current round remain eligible for the next round.
6

Result Announcement

Final results follow a mock allotment list to help set expectations.

AACCC publishes the final result for the round. Before that, a mock seat allotment result list is released so students can estimate their chances of admission to their desired college.
7

Reporting & Document Verification

Report to your college in person and complete admission formalities.

Candidates report to the designated college in person, submit required documents, and pay admission fees. The authoritative body verifies documents to confirm the seat. Seats of candidates who don't report within the stipulated time are considered vacant for the next round.

Karnataka AYUSH Counselling 2025 Process

What You Need to Know

  • KEA counselling begins once NEET-UG results are declared.
  • KEA conducts BAMS counselling for government, private, management and NRI quota seats across Karnataka.
  • Students must complete registration on the official KEA website to be eligible.

The KEA process runs across 4 rounds:

01 · Round 1 02 · Round 2 03 · Mop-Up Round 04 · Stray Vacancy Round
The Mop-Up Round runs only if seats remain unfilled after Rounds 1 & 2. Stray Vacancy Rounds may follow if seats still remain unfilled after the Mop-Up Round.

KEA NEET-UG BAMS Counselling 2025 Stages

The KEA NEET-UG BAMS counselling takes place in various rounds. Each round has multiple stages, which are explained below.

1

Online Registration & Fee Payment

Register on the KEA website and pay the fee to complete this stage.

Interested and eligible candidates register through the Karnataka Ayush counselling website within the stipulated time. No documents or printouts need to be sent to prove eligibility. Candidates found ineligible at any stage have their candidature cancelled immediately. Registration is complete once the fee is paid online via card.
2

Document Verification

Get documents verified and download your verification slip.

Students must get documents verified within the stipulated time and download the verification slip once complete. No message or reminder is sent about the verification date/time — candidates must track this themselves. Missing the window means losing eligibility for the next stage, option entry.
3

Option Entry

Enter your college preferences in order — no limit on the number of options.

Candidates who clear document verification enter their most preferred college first, then continue in order of preference. There's no limit on the number of colleges added. The order matters: selected options remain for the current and subsequent rounds, though they can be reordered or deleted next round. New colleges/seats added to the matrix can be chosen in the upcoming round's option entry.
4

Mock Seat Allotment

A preview allotment to help you gauge your chances and reorder preferences.

KEA publishes mock allotment results based on current preferences, helping candidates understand which seats they might be eligible for and reorder accordingly. Results can be viewed by entering your CET number on the KEA website. Final results may differ from the mock allotment.
5

Seat Allotment & Results

Seats are allotted by rank, preference and reservation rules — in 3 phases.

Seats are allotted based on rank/merit, preferences, and government reservation rules. Every round follows the same 3-phase structure — see the breakdown below.

All Candidates Eligible

Phase 1 begins with Special Category Seats allotted to Karnataka candidates in this order:

People with Disabilities (PwD)NCC CandidatesSports Candidates

After Special Category seats, all candidates — regardless of category — are first considered for General Merit (GM) seats. Those not allotted a GM seat are then considered for their respective category based on rank and preferences. This order repeats until allotments stop changing.

Reserved-Category Candidates Only

  • Applies only to candidates from reserved categories.
  • Unfilled seats in the rural and Kannada-medium quota of reservation categories convert to general seats within the same reserved category — securable only by candidates of that category.
  • Allotment again starts with Special Category seats, in the same PwD → NCC → Sports order, this time for Reserved-category candidates.
  • After that, remaining reserved-category candidates are considered for remaining seats in their category.
  • This order repeats until no candidate's allotment changes.

All Candidates Eligible Again

  • Reserved general-category seats and Special Category seats unfilled in Phase 2 convert to General Merit seats. If no general-category seats remain, rural/Kannada-medium quota seats convert to general seats of the respective reserved category instead.
  • Converted general merit seats go to all GM candidates plus other reserved-category candidates. Converted category-general seats go only to candidates of that same category.
  • Allotment again opens with Special Category seats — PwD → NCC → Sports.
  • Remaining seats then go to all candidates by rank and preference, regardless of category.
  • Any unfilled reserved seats convert automatically to GM and are re-offered — repeating until no reserved seats remain and allotments stop changing.

Results for each round are published at the end of the round. Candidates allotted a seat in Phase 1 or 2 must wait until the full round completes before deciding on next steps.
